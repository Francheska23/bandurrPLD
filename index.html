What is the Bandurria?

The Bandurria is a traditional Filipino string instrument, widely used in rondalla music. With its unique timbre, it brings a distinct flavor to Filipino cultural music.

A Brief History

The Bandurria is believed to have originated from Spain and was brought to the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period. It is a member of the lute family and has evolved into an essential part of Filipino folk music, especially in rondalla groups.

Bandurria Features

music_note Unique Sound

The Bandurria produces a crisp, clear tone that is distinct from other string instruments, contributing a unique layer to Filipino music.

music_note String Configuration

It typically has 14 strings, arranged in 7 courses, with each pair of strings tuned to the same pitch.

The Bandurria in Filipino Music

The Bandurria is often featured in Filipino folk music, particularly in rondalla performances. It plays an essential role in creating the characteristic harmony and rhythm of the ensemble.
